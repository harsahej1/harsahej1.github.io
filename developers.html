FromSoftware

FromSoftware is a Japanese video game developer known for creating some of the most challenging and critically acclaimed games in recent years. The company was founded in November 1986 by Naotoshi Jin, and has since developed numerous titles for a variety of platforms.

FromSoftware's games are known for their high difficulty and emphasis on exploration and discovery. The company's most famous franchises include the Soulsborne series, which includes the Demon's Souls, Dark Souls, and Bloodborne games, as well as Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice.

George R. R. Martin

George R. R. Martin is an American author and television producer, best known for his series of epic fantasy novels, A Song of Ice and Fire. The series was adapted into the hit HBO television series Game of Thrones, which aired from 2011 to 2019.

Martin is known for his meticulous attention to detail and world-building, and has won numerous awards for his writing. He was brought on as a consultant for Elden Ring, and has worked closely with FromSoftware to create a rich and immersive game world.

Hidetaka Miyazaki

Hidetaka Miyazaki is a Japanese video game designer and director, and the president of FromSoftware. Miyazaki joined the company in 2004, and has since worked on some of FromSoftware's most popular and influential games.

Miyazaki is known for his innovative approach to game design, and has been praised for his ability to create immersive and challenging game worlds. He has been the director of numerous FromSoftware games, including Demon's Souls, Dark Souls, and Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice.
